Road Traffic Statistics

According to the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), road traffic deaths in the Philippines increased by 39% from 7,938 in 2011 to 11,096 in 2021.

Here's a more detailed breakdown:

  • 2011: 7,938 road traffic deaths
  • 2021: 11,096 road traffic deaths
  • Increase: 39%
  • Factors: The increase in road traffic deaths coincides with the end of lockdowns and mobility restrictions.
  • Cost: Road traffic injuries cost about 2.6% of the country's Gross Domestic Product (GDP).
  • Approximately 1.35 million people die in road crashes each year. (World Health Organization, 2023)
  • 20-50 million people suffer non-fatal injuries, often with long-term disabilities. (World Health Organization, 2023)
  • Speeding contributes to nearly 30% of all fatal accidents. (National Highway Traffic Safety Administration, 2022)
  • Studies also show that for every 1% increase in speed, the risk of a fatal crash increases by 4%. (International Transport Forum, 2021)
